Oven-Roasted Caponata

Meagan Houle

Recommended Wine: Due Vigne Nebbiolo

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-size eggplants (about 1 1/2 pounds), ends trimmed, cut in half lengthwise
  • 1.4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1/2 cup coarsely chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup coarsely chopped celery
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1/4 cup Balsamic Vinegar Syrup or 2 tablespoons each balsamic vinegar and firmly packed light brown sugar
  • One 15.5 ounce can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 teaspoons chopped fresh basil
  • 1/2 cup drained and chopped pitted green olives
  • 1/4 cup drained and chopped capers
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh Italian parsley

Preparation

  1. Preheat the oven to 400F and line baking sheet with silicone liner or aluminum foil.
  2. Drizzle the cut sides of the eggplant with a little olive oil, and lay, cut side down, on the baking sheet.
  3. Place the garlic on the baking sheet and drizzle with some of the oil, reserving the remainder for the next step.
  4. Bake the garlic and eggplant until the eggplant is tender, 25 to 30 minutes: a knife should pierce the skin easily, with no resistance.
  5. Remove from the oven and allow to cool. Then peel off the skin of the eggplant and coarsely chop the flesh.
  6. In a large skillet, heat the remaining olive oil over medium heat and add the eggplant.
  7. Squeeze the garlic from the skin into the skillet and cook the mixture, stirring, for 1 to 2 minutes, tossing to coast with oil.
  8. Add the onion, celery, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if using and cook, stirring, until the onion and celery begin to soften a bit, 3 to 4 minutes.
  9. Add the balsamic syrup, tomatoes, and basil and simmer the mixture for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  10. Remove from the heat and stir in the olives, capers, and parsley. Allow the caponata to cool to room temperature, then taste and adjust the seasonings.

Chicken Piccata

Nonni Fadelli

Recommended Wine: Due Vigne Sauvignon Blanc

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 2 extra-large eggs
  • 3/4 cup seasoned dry breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Romano cheese
  • olive oil
  • 4 tsp capers
  • 3 tablespoons butter at room temperature
  • 1/3 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice (2 lemons)
  • 1/2 cup Due Vigne Chardonnay
  • Sliced lemon and chopped fresh parsley, for serving

Preparation

  1. Pound chicken breasts to about ¼-inch thick.
  2. Mix the flour, salt, and pepper in a shallow plate.
  3. In a second plate, beat the egg and ½ tablespoon of water.
  4. Mix the breadcrumbs and cheese in a third plate.
  5. Dip each piece of chicken first in the flour, then dip in the egg and breadcrumb mixtures.
  6. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large sauté pan over medium to medium-low heat.
  7. Add the chicken breasts and cook for 4 minutes on each side, until browned.
  8. Place them on a plate, cover and set aside.
  9. In the same pan, melt 1 tablespoon of the butter, then add the lemon juice and wine.
  10. Bring to a boil over high heat, about 2 minutes.
  11. Remove from heat and add the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter and capers: swirl to combine.

Serve one chicken breast on each plate. Spoon the sauce over the chicken and serve with a slice of lemon and a sprinkling of fresh parsley.

Steaks on the Grill with Grilled Asparagus

Becky & Ed Ploeg

Recommended Wine: Due Vigne Cabernet

Michigan residents and Wine Club members Becky and Ed Ploeg share a New Year’s Day tradition that includes Due Vigne’s award-winning Cabernet:

"Our very favorite Cabernet meal is steaks on the grill with grilled asparagus, and no mater how high the snow is piled up on the deck, we clear a path to the grill and open a bottle of Due Vigne Cabernet.

Everyone has their favorite way to prep a steak, but here is how we do the asparagus."

Ingredients

  • Fresh asparagus, trimmed and rinsed
  • Olive oil
  • Montreal steak seasoning
  • Salt
  • Minced garlic

Preparation

  1. Place trimmed and rinsed asparagus on a plate or in a Ziploc bag.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle steak seasoning liberally.
  3. Add salt and garlic to taste, toss together and grill until tender-crisp and bright green.

If cooking inside, place on foil lined baking sheet and roast in 400 degree oven for 8-12 minutes. (You can do the exact same thing with fresh green beans, very good too.)

Oregon Chinook Salmon with Pesto

Anna Alexander

Recommended Wine: Due Vigne Nebbiolo

Pesto Sauce

  • 3 cups loosely packed fresh basil
  • 3/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 3 large garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated parmesan cheese
  • 3 Tbs. Romano pecorino cheese or parmesan

Put basil, oil. pine nuts, garlic and salt into blender or food processor. Process until smooth. Pour sauce into small bowl. Add parmesan and romano cheeses and mix to blend. Taste and adjust for seasoning. Makes 1 cup of sauce. Enough for 3/4 to 1 lb. of fresh pasta. Linguine or fettuccine are best. When mixing with pasta add a Tbs. of the pasta water to sauce to mix well.

Salmon

  • Four 6-8 oz. fresh chinook salmon fillets with skin on
  • Rub with olive oil and a little lemon juice
  • sprinkle with salt and pepper and a dash paprika
  • Add 2 thin lemons slices on top. Set on foil and barbecue or bake
